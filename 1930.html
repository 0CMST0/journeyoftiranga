Civil Disobedience Movement: launched to break the Salt law

A public declaration was made on 26 January 1930 - a day which the Congress Party urged Indians to celebrate as 'Independence Day'. The declaration was passed due to the breakdown of negotiations between leaders of the freedom movement and the British over the question of dominion status for India. 1929, Lord Irwin, the then Viceroy of India, announced the “Irwin Declaration” wherein India would be granted dominion status.

Under pressure, Lord Irwin, at a meeting with Jinnah, Nehru, Gandhi and Sapru said he couldn't promise dominion status anymore and Instead passed the 'PurnaSwaraj' resolution that called for complete independence. This marked the beginning of a large scale political movement against colonial rule.

Lord Irwin

The Salt Satyagraha was a mass civil disobedience movement initiated by Mahatma Gandhi against the salt tax imposed by the British government in India. He led a large group of people from Sabarmati Ashram on 12th March 1930 till Dandi, a coastal village in Gujarat, to break the salt law by producing salt from seawater.

Indians had been making salt from seawater free of cost until the passing of the 1882 Salt Act that gave the British monopoly over the production of salt and authority to impose a salt tax. It was a criminal offence to violate the salt act. The salt tax accounted for 8.2% of the British Raj revenue from tax and Gandhiji knew that the government could not ignore this. He would lead a group of people from his Ashram at Sabarmati on 12th March 1930 and walk through the villages of Gujarat. Around 60,000 people including Gandhiji himself were arrested by the government. The government tried to suppress it by introducing more laws. Thousands of women also took part in the Satyagraha.Foreign clothes were boycotted. Liqueur shops were picketed. There were strikes all over.

This movement had three main effects:

1)It pushed Indian freedom struggle into the limelight in western media.

2)It brought a lot of people including women and the depressed classes directly in touch with the freedom movement.

3)It showed the power of the non-violent Satyagraha as a tool in fighting imperialism.

Vedaranyam march led by C Rajagopalachari:

C Rajagopalachari led the Salt Satyagraha protest in Tamil Nadu. Inspired by the Dandi March by Mahatma Gandhi, a group of 100 men led by C Rajagopalachari marched to Vedaranyam and extracted salt from seawater in April 1930 to break the salt law imposed by the British.
